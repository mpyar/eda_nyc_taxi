The **Congestion Surcharge** is another fixed fee in NYC taxi trips, separate from the improvement surcharge:

---

## 🚦 Congestion Surcharge Overview
- **Definition**: A flat fee added to trips that begin, end, or pass through certain parts of Manhattan (below 96th Street).
- **Amount**:
  - **$2.75** for yellow taxis.
  - **$2.50** for green taxis and for-hire vehicles (like Uber/Lyft).
  - **$0.75 per passenger** for pooled rides.
- **Purpose**: Introduced in 2019 to reduce traffic congestion in Manhattan and generate revenue for the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA).
- **Application**: Automatically added to the fare whenever the trip qualifies, regardless of distance or duration.

---

## 📊 Role in Data Analysis
- **Revenue Impact**: It’s a significant surcharge compared to the $0.30 improvement fee, so it noticeably increases passenger costs.
- **Geographic Sensitivity**: Analysts often filter trips by pickup/drop-off zones to measure how much congestion surcharge contributes to overall fare totals.
- **Policy Insight**: Since drivers don’t keep this surcharge (it goes to the MTA), it’s important to separate driver earnings from passenger costs in analysis.
- **Behavioral Effect**: Researchers study whether the surcharge discourages short trips in Manhattan or shifts demand toward subways and buses.

---

## 🔑 Key Difference
- **Improvement Surcharge**: $0.30 flat fee, applies to all rides.
- **Congestion Surcharge**: $2.75 (yellow taxis), applies only to Manhattan trips, much larger impact on fare totals.
