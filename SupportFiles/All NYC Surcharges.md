Here’s a clear side‑by‑side comparison of the main NYC taxi surcharges:

| **Surcharge**            | **Amount**                          | **Applies To**                                                                 | **Purpose**                                                                 |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Improvement Surcharge** | $0.30 flat fee                      | Every yellow taxi trip                                                          | Funds taxi system upgrades, accessibility, and technology improvements       |
| **Congestion Surcharge**  | $2.75 (yellow taxis)<br>$2.50 (green taxis/FHV)<br>$0.75 per passenger (pooled rides) | Trips that begin, end, or pass through Manhattan below 96th Street             | Reduce traffic congestion and generate revenue for the MTA                   |
| **MTA State Surcharge**   | $0.50 flat fee                      | Every yellow taxi trip                                                          | Direct revenue to the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA)            |
| **Airport Access Fee**    | $1.25–$1.75 (varies by airport and vehicle type) | Trips to/from JFK, LaGuardia, Newark airports                                   | Support airport infrastructure and operations                                |

---

### 🔎 Key Takeaways
- **Flat vs. Variable**: Improvement and MTA surcharges are small flat fees on every trip, while congestion and airport fees depend on location.
- **Impact on Fare**: Congestion surcharge is the largest and most noticeable for passengers, especially on short Manhattan trips.
- **Revenue Destination**: Improvement surcharge → TLC funds; Congestion & MTA surcharge → MTA; Airport fees → airport authorities.
